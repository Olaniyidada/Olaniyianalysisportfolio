Deluxe Incorporated
Expense report management process

Deluxe Incorporated's AS-IS expense report management process was analyzed using a swim lane flowchart. The companyâ€™s expense reimbursement is manually processed by employees, and the expense report package is hand-delivered to the manager for review and approval.
The process goes back and forth until it reaches the senior manager for an approval limit above $1,000, and finally stops at the accounting department for compliance and reimbursement to compensate the employee's travel expenses.
This method seems rather outdated, given that many organizations are moving towards a paperless office environment. The manual recording of information is prone to human errors such as using the wrong cost center, missing information, challenges of poor handwriting, and the need for printing hard copies of the expense report and receipts, which requires office equipment resources. Manual rework requires time and costs, and there is no guarantee that errors will be fixed the first time. There is a need for improvement, digitization, and automation of the expense report management process.

Agile Scrum
Methodology Simplified

Agile Scrum is a framework for managing and completing complex projects. It emphasizes iterative progress through time-boxed sprints, typically lasting 1-4 weeks. Scrum teams are cross-functional, including roles like the Product Owner, Scrum Master, and Development Team. The process begins with a prioritized product backlog, which informs sprint planning. Daily stand-up meetings, or Scrums, ensure transparency and quick issue resolution. At the end of each sprint, a review and retrospective are conducted to assess progress and improve processes. Agile Scrum promotes flexibility, continuous improvement, and close collaboration, aiming to deliver high-quality, incremental product enhancements that meet user needs.

Unilever Canteen
Ordering System [Web page]

This capstone project focuses on Unilever, a British-Dutch FMCG company. Unilever has 1,500 employees in its UK offices, served by two canteens that each seat 150 people. The peak lunch hour between 12 and 1 pm causes congestion, leading to long waits for tables and food. To address this, stakeholders were engaged, and an online canteen ordering system was proposed. This system allows employees to order food online, have it delivered to their workstations, and pay via payroll deduction. It also enables feedback on food quality and improves inventory management. The Jira software development tool will be used for webpage development.
